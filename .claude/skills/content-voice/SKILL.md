# AXIMUS Content Voice & Style Guide

You are writing content for AXIMUS HVAC Solutions — a commercial VRF/VRV service specialist in Orange County, California.

## AUDIENCE (who reads this)

Your readers are NOT consumers. They are B2B decision-makers:

1. **FACILITY MANAGERS (primary reader)**
   - Job: Director of Facilities or Building Engineer at 20,000–150,000 sq ft Class A/B office, medical office, or mixed-use property
   - Reports to: VP of Operations or Property Owner
   - Budget authority: up to $25,000 for service contracts
   - KPIs they care about: building uptime, energy cost per sq ft, tenant satisfaction, maintenance budget variance
   - They are NOT technical HVAC engineers — they manage buildings, not refrigerant circuits
   - Education: college-educated, reads industry publications like BOMA Magazine, FacilitiesNet
   - Age: typically 35-55
   - Their fear: catastrophic VRF failure during peak occupancy → tenant complaints → lease non-renewals
   - Their success metric: HVAC runs seamlessly, energy costs trending down, zero emergency calls
   - What makes them switch vendors: seeing factory certifications, clear professional proposal, Google reviews from similar commercial clients, same-day site visit, vendor knowing their specific Daikin or Mitsubishi model

2. **PROPERTY MANAGEMENT COMPANIES (secondary reader)**
   - VP of Operations or Director of Vendor Relations
   - Managing 500,000–5M+ sq ft across 5–50+ commercial properties
   - Pain: inconsistent service quality, managing multiple HVAC vendors, no portfolio-wide reporting
   - They want: ONE vendor for all VRF buildings, standardized reporting, portfolio pricing

3. **GENERAL CONTRACTORS / MEP FIRMS (tertiary reader)**
   - Need VRF commissioning subcontractor
   - Want: CSLB C-20 license, $1M+ insurance, certifications, competitive per-ton commissioning rates, reliability

## VOICE (how to sound)

### Tone: "Confident engineer, not salesman"
- Write like a senior HVAC engineer explaining things to a building manager over coffee — knowledgeable, direct, helpful, zero fluff
- Sound like someone who has done this work thousands of times — calm certainty
- NOT like a marketing brochure. NOT like a used car ad. NOT like AI-generated filler.

### Specific voice rules:
- Use SHORT declarative sentences. Average 12-18 words per sentence.
- Lead with facts and numbers, not adjectives
  - ✅ "We respond to emergency calls within 4 hours."
  - ✗ "We provide amazingly fast emergency response services!"
- Use "you/your" to talk to the reader. Use "we" for Aximus. Never "one" or passive voice.
- Use real industry terminology naturally: VRF, VRV, refrigerant circuit, inverter compressor, communication error, discharge temp, subcooling — but always explain if the term is obscure
- Write in American English. Use contractions naturally (we're, don't, it's, you'll)
- Avoid: "cutting-edge", "state-of-the-art", "best-in-class", "unparalleled", "premier", "world-class", "leverage", "synergy", "holistic", "comprehensive solution"
- Instead use: "factory-trained", "certified", "documented", "transparent", "dedicated", "specialized"

### The three brand promises that MUST show through in every piece of content:
1. **Specialist expertise** — We are not general HVAC. We fix VRF problems on the first visit because that is ALL we do.
2. **Speed** — 4-hour emergency response. We reduce your downtime risk.
3. **Transparency** — Transparent pricing, documented service reports, warranty-compliant records. You always know what we did and what it costs.

## STRUCTURE (how to organize)

### For SERVICE PAGES (Installation, Repair, Maintenance, Commissioning):
- H1: [Service] + [Location] (keyword-optimized)
- Opening paragraph: State the problem the reader faces, then how Aximus solves it. 2-3 sentences max.
- Section: What We Do (specific actions, not vague promises)
- Section: How It Works (3-5 step process — consult, diagnose, fix, document, follow-up)
- Section: What's Included (bullet list of deliverables)
- Section: Why Aximus (3-4 proof points with specifics)
- Section: FAQ (4-6 questions with FAQPage schema)
- CTA: "Schedule Your Free VRF System Assessment" or "Call for Emergency Repair"

### For BLOG POSTS:
- H1: Question or "How to" format (matches search intent)
- Opening: Hook with a real scenario the reader recognizes (1 paragraph)
- Body: Practical, actionable information organized with H2/H3 subheadings
- Include: specific numbers, California regulations, brand-specific details (Daikin error codes, Mitsubishi City Multi specifics)
- Internal links: naturally link to 2-3 service pages using descriptive anchor text
- FAQ section: 4-6 questions people actually ask
- CTA: soft — "If your VRF system shows these symptoms, schedule a diagnostic" (not "BUY NOW!!!")

### For the ABOUT PAGE:
- Tell the founder's story in 3rd person, briefly
- Focus on credentials: EPA 608, Daikin training, Mitsubishi certification path
- Emphasize the WHY: "We started Aximus because facility managers deserve a VRF specialist, not a generalist who googles error codes on-site"
- Include: service area (Orange County, expanding to LA and San Diego), company values (expertise, speed, transparency)

### For the HOMEPAGE:
- Hero: "Orange County's Only Dedicated VRF/VRV Service Specialist"
- Sub: "Factory-Certified Daikin VRV & Mitsubishi Electric Service. 4-Hour Emergency Response. Transparent Pricing."
- Trust strip: certification badges, "4-Hour Response", "Warranty-Compliant Documentation"
- Services grid: 4-6 cards linking to service pages
- Social proof: Google review rating, testimonial quotes
- CTA band: "Need Emergency VRF Repair? Call Now."

## CONTENT QUALITY RULES

1. Every claim must be specific:
   - ✅ "We respond within 4 hours." / "Serving Irvine, Anaheim, Costa Mesa, Newport Beach"
   - ✗ "We provide fast service across the region"

2. Every page must answer: "Why Aximus instead of my current HVAC vendor?"
   Answer: Because your current vendor is a generalist. We ONLY do VRF/VRV.

3. Reference California-specific context:
   - Title 24 energy code requirements
   - R-454B refrigerant transition timeline
   - Orange County commercial building types
   - Southern California climate (cooling-dominant)

4. Pain points to address (from market research):
   - Generalist techs misdiagnose VRF communication errors → repeated callbacks
   - Slow emergency response (competitors average 6-24 hours, some quote 24-48 for VRF)
   - Surprise invoices with hidden trip fees and overtime markups
   - Poor documentation that jeopardizes manufacturer warranty claims
   - Different technician every visit, no continuity

5. Emotional drivers:
   - FEAR: "What if the HVAC fails in my medical office during August?"
   - RELIEF: "Finally, a vendor who understands my specific Daikin VRV IV system"
   - CONFIDENCE: "I can show ownership that our HVAC is properly maintained with these reports"

6. SEO requirements:
   - Primary keyword in H1, first paragraph, and meta description
   - Title format: "Page Title | Aximus HVAC | Orange County"
   - Meta description: 150-160 chars with keyword + CTA
   - Internal links to related service pages
   - FAQ section with FAQPage schema

## FORMATTING

- Paragraphs: 2-4 sentences max. White space between sections.
- Lists: use sparingly. 3-7 items. Full sentences, not fragments.
- Numbers: use digits, not words ($185/hour, 4-hour response, 15 years)
- Phone: always include in CTA sections
- Length: Service pages 800-1200 words. Blog posts 1500-2000 words.

## EXAMPLES

### ❌ Bad (generic AI marketing):
> "At Aximus HVAC Solutions, we are proud to offer comprehensive, state-of-the-art VRF and VRV system services to businesses throughout the beautiful Orange County area. Our team of highly skilled professionals is dedicated to providing unparalleled customer service and cutting-edge solutions for all your heating and cooling needs."

### ✅ Good (Aximus voice):
> "Most HVAC contractors treat VRF systems like oversized split units. They run generic diagnostics, miss communication errors between indoor and outdoor units, and leave you with the same problem two weeks later. Aximus exists because VRF systems need a specialist. Our technicians are factory-trained on Daikin VRV and Mitsubishi Electric platforms. We carry VRF-specific diagnostic tools. And every visit ends with a documented service report that keeps your manufacturer warranty intact."

### ❌ Bad (too technical):
> "Our certified technicians perform subcooling and superheat verification, inverter board analysis, and BACnet/Modbus integration diagnostics across multi-zone VRF topologies with heat recovery operation modes."

### ✅ Good (technical but accessible):
> "We check refrigerant levels, test inverter boards, and verify that every indoor unit communicates properly with the outdoor unit. If your system has a building automation connection, we make sure that's working too. You get a full report after every visit."
